Enemies: A Tale for Lads and Lasses

A movie poster concept balancing mood and narrative tension, using layered type and a restrained palette to set the tone.

RolePoster & Type Design
ToolsPhotoshop, Illustrator
Year2024
01

The Brief

What the project set out to do, and the story behind the design.

Process

Work on this poster started with document illustration concepts and a moodboard exploring tone — tension, distance, and an unresolved conflict between two figures. From there, type layering and a restrained colour palette were tested to find a composition that read as dramatic without tipping into melodrama.

Challenge

The hardest part was suggesting narrative tension with almost no information — no tagline, no cast, just two silhouettes and a title. Getting the framing, negative space, and type hierarchy to carry that weight took several rounds of composition testing before the poster felt like it was telling a story rather than just looking moody.

Outcome

The final poster uses layered type and a restrained palette to set the tone before a viewer ever sees a trailer, letting the composition do the storytelling on its own — one of the more editorially considered pieces in this portfolio.
