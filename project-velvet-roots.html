Velvet Roots

A wine packaging system designed to feel rich and tactile on the shelf, with label details that reward a second look.

RolePackaging Design
ToolsIllustrator, Photoshop
Year2025
01

The Brief

What the project set out to do, and the story behind the brand.

Process

Velvet Roots began with research into how wine packaging communicates quality on a shelf full of competitors. From there, the work moved through label sketches, material studies, and a custom dieline before settling on a structural box design that felt substantial in hand. A deep red palette and bold typographic treatment were chosen to signal richness without leaning on cliché vineyard imagery.

Challenge

The hardest part was balancing tactile, premium detail with something that could realistically be produced as a working dieline. Early concepts looked great flat but didn't hold up structurally — several rounds of prototyping were needed to get proportions, fold lines, and label placement working together as one physical object, not just a 2D design.

Outcome

The final packaging system feels rich and tactile on the shelf, with details that reward a closer look. It sets the bottle apart from typical wine packaging while staying realistic to produce, and became one of the strongest packaging pieces in this portfolio.
